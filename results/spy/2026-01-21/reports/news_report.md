### **Weekly Market & Macroeconomic Report: January 14–21, 2026**

#### **1. Executive Summary: The Davos Relief and the "Greenland Shock"**

The week was characterized by intense geopolitical volatility followed by a
fragile recovery. The "Greenland Shock"—President Trump’s threat of 10%–25%
tariffs on European allies linked to the acquisition of Greenland—triggered a
sharp 700-point Dow sell-off on January 20. However, markets rebounded on
January 21 after the President’s Davos speech eased immediate tariff rhetoric,
though structural trade tensions and a Department of Justice (DOJ) probe into
the Federal Reserve have left investors on edge.

#### **2. Geopolitics & Trade: The New Front in the Atlantic**

* **The Greenland Ultimatum:** The U.S. administration has signaled a February 1
  deadline for a "framework" regarding Greenland, threatening a 10% tariff on
  goods from Denmark, Norway, Sweden, France, Germany, the UK, Netherlands, and
  Finland. While currently "paused," this creates a massive "gray swan" event
  for Q1.
* **EU Retaliation:** The European Union is reportedly preparing retaliatory
  measures. Analysts warn that a full-scale trade war could add 0.4% to
  inflation and disrupt the $1.6T Treasury holdings of European entities.
* **Davos Sentiment:** The 2026 World Economic Forum was dominated by
  discussions on "de-globalization" and "token factories." Trump’s speech
  focused on U.S. economic dominance but notable silence on NATO support until
  late in his address added to the climate of "governance uncertainty."

#### **3. Monetary Policy: The Fed Under Fire**

* **DOJ Investigation:** In a move that shocked markets, the DOJ launched an
  investigation into Fed Chair Jerome Powell. This has intensified fears
  regarding the central bank’s independence and the potential for the executive
  branch to exert more direct control over interest rate decisions.
* **Yield Curve & Bond Volatility:** The 10-year Treasury yield climbed above
  4.20%, marking a multi-month high. Despite this, the MOVE index (bond
  volatility) remains at multi-year lows, suggesting that the market may be
  drastically underestimating the risk of a "bond rout."
* **Liquidity Shifts:** The Fed has pivoted toward a new form of quantitative
  easing (QE), injecting roughly $40B in liquidity—a move Jamie Dimon (JPM)
  noted is finding its way directly into asset prices, supporting the S&P 500
  despite macro headwinds.

#### **4. Macro Indicators: Inflation Sticking, Growth Diverging**

* **Inflation (CPI):** December CPI rose 0.3% (2.7% YoY), while Core CPI sits at
  2.6%. While disinflation continues in rents, the 2.7% headline remains
  stubbornly above the Fed's 2% target, complicating the path for 2026 rate
  cuts.
* **Employment:** December job growth was disappointing, with initial
  unemployment claims hitting 264,000. Critics argue that without
  government-subsidized industries, employment growth would be negative.
* **The "Goldilocks" Theory:** ARK’s Cathie Wood is forecasting 5% GDP growth
  for 2026, driven by an AI-led productivity boom. This contrasts sharply with
  mainstream fears of a "Sell America" trade where stocks, bonds, and the USD
  are sold simultaneously.

#### **5. Market Dynamics & The "Great Rotation"**

* **S&P 500 (SPY) vs. Small Caps (IWM):** Early 2026 is seeing a significant
  rotation. While the S&P 500 reached a record high of 6,977 on Jan 12, it has
  since stalled. Small caps (IWM) are up 8% YTD, outperforming large-cap tech as
  investors seek value outside the "Magnificent Seven."
* **AI Bubble Concerns:** Despite TSMC’s strong earnings and a $56B AI
  investment plan, sentiment is shifting. Investors are identifying "weak links"
  in the AI chain—companies where productivity gains haven't yet translated to
  the bottom line.
* **Concentration Risk:** The top three mega-cap tech stocks now control over
  20% of the S&P 500's value, leading many traders to move toward equal-weight
  indexes or mid-cap ETFs (MDY).

#### **6. Safe Havens: Gold & Silver at "Wartime" Levels**

Precious metals continue to be the star performers of the cycle. Gold has
brushed $4,600, and the gold/silver ratio is behaving in a manner typical of
major geopolitical conflicts. This reflects a deep-seated fear of currency
debasement and trade-war-induced inflation.
