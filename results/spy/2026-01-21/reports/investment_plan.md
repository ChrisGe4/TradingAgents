### The Investment Plan

Here is how we are going to handle this.

**1. The Rationale**
I’m siding with the Bear here because of one specific concept: **Overhead
Supply.** The Bear noted that the Volume Weighted Moving Average (VWMA) is
at $688, while we are trading at $677. That means the average recent buyer is
losing money. As soon as the price bumps up to $680 or $685, those people are
going to sell to get out at breakeven. That creates a concrete ceiling that "
cash on Apple's balance sheet" cannot magically break through in the short term.

Furthermore, the Bull is ignoring the **DOJ probe into the Fed**. Markets hate
uncertainty, but they *loathe* threats to Central Bank independence. That is a
structural risk that justifies a lower valuation multiple (P/E compression),
regardless of how good Microsoft's earnings are.

**2. Learning from Past Mistakes**
Looking back at my own track record, I've been burned in situations exactly like
this. In previous dips, I looked at "oversold" Bollinger Bands and bought
immediately, thinking I was outsmarting the crowd. I ignored the fact that the
trend had broken (the 50-day line). I learned the hard way that **indicators
like Bollinger Bands work great in ranges, but they kill you in trend reversals.
**

When institutions are liquidating (as shown by the sharp RSI drop), "oversold"
can stay oversold for weeks while the price drifts lower. I’m not making the
mistake of catching a falling knife again just because the fundamentals look
pretty 12 months out.
