# SPY Weekly Analysis Report: Geopolitics, The "Greenland Shock," and Market Rotation (Jan 14 – Jan 21, 2026)

The past week for the **SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust (SPY)** was a volatile
masterclass in how geopolitical headlines can temporarily hijack a bull market
fueled by AI and liquidity. After reaching record highs earlier in the month,
the SPY faced a significant stress test driven by trade rhetoric, sticky
inflation data, and a notable shift in market leadership.

---

### 1. The "Greenland Shock" and Geopolitical Volatility

The defining theme of the week was the "Greenland Ultimatum." President Trump’s
announcement of a potential 10% tariff on several European nations (including
Denmark, France, and Germany)—rising to 25% by June—sparked a sharp "Sell
America" trade on **Tuesday, January 20th**.

* **Market Impact:** The Dow Jones dived 700 points, and the SPY fell 1.4% in
  pre-market trading as investors priced in a full-scale U.S.-EU trade war.
* **The Rebound:** By **Wednesday, January 21st**, the narrative shifted. In his
  speech at Davos, Trump "paused" the Greenland tariffs, easing near-term tail
  risk. This led to a relief rally, with the SPY recovering ~0.57% as the "
  Framework" of the deal took center stage, though long-term uncertainty
  remains.

### 2. Macroeconomic Backdrop: Sticky Inflation and Fed Pressure

The economic data released during the week presented a "Goldilocks with Thorns"
scenario.

* **CPI Data:** December CPI came in at 2.7% (headline) and 2.6% (core). While
  disinflation is persisting, it remains above the Fed's 2% target, leading to "
  sticky" inflation concerns.
* **The Powell/DOJ Factor:** Markets were rattled by news of a potential
  Department of Justice (DOJ) probe into Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell.
  Despite this political pressure, the Fed appears to be pivoting toward a form
  of "Not QE" (Quantitative Easing), injecting roughly $40 billion in liquidity,
  which Jamie Dimon (JPM) noted is providing a floor for asset prices.
* **Yield Curve:** The 10-year Treasury yield surged above 4.20%, signaling
  unease. Bond volatility (MOVE Index) remains at multi-year lows, which some
  analysts warn suggests that risk is being underestimated.

### 3. The "Great Rotation" of 2026

A significant internal shift is occurring within the S&P 500. The "Magnificent
Seven" tech giants, which dominated 2025, are beginning to stall as investors
seek value elsewhere.

* **Small-Caps (IWM) vs. Large-Caps (SPY):** Small-caps have outperformed by a
  wide margin in the first three weeks of January. Analysts are calling this
  the "Great Rotation of 2026," as lower borrowing rates and potential tax
  breaks favor smaller, domestic-focused companies.
* **AI Resilience:** Despite the rotation, AI remains a core pillar. TSMC’s
  strong earnings and $56B AI investment plan provided a mid-week boost to the
  tech sector, proving that the AI secular bull market is still fundamentally
  sound even if valuations are being reset.

### 4. Technical Analysis and Sentiment

Technically, the SPY is exhibiting a "wedge pattern," which often precedes a
trend reversal.

* **Key Levels:** Analysts are watching the **$685** level closely. A break
  below this could signal a double-digit correction.
* **Sentiment:** The AAII Sentiment Survey showed bullish sentiment jumping to
  49.5% mid-week, but the sudden tariff-induced sell-off on Jan 20th suggests
  that "greed" can quickly turn into "fear" in a headline-driven environment.

---

### Insights for Traders and Investors

* **For Short-Term Traders:** The "Davos Relief" may be short-lived. Volatility
  is expected to remain high as the February 1st tariff deadline approaches. Use
  the $685–$690 range as a critical pivot zone for stop-losses.
* **For Long-Term Investors:** The liquidity injection from the Fed ("Not QE")
  and strong corporate earnings (especially in AI and Banking) suggest the
  primary bull trend is intact. However, diversification into small-caps (IWM)
  or value-oriented ETFs (SCHD) may provide better risk-adjusted returns in the
  current "rotation" environment.
* **Safe Havens:** Gold and silver are signaling "wartime behavior," hitting new
  highs. Maintaining a small allocation to precious metals or defensive
  covered-call ETFs (like SPIN or BXMX) could hedge against a geopolitical
  escalation.
