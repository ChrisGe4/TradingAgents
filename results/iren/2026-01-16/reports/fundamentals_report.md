**Comprehensive Fundamental Report for IREN**
*(As of 2026-01-16, based on available data up to Q1 2026 ending September 30,
2025)*

This report provides an in-depth analysis of IREN's financial health,
performance, and cash flow activities, drawing insights from its annual
financial statement for the fiscal year 2025 (ended June 30, 2025) and its most
recent quarterly report for Q1 2026 (ended September 30, 2025).

**1. Company Financial History (Annual - Fiscal Year 2025 ended June 30, 2025)**

**Income Statement Analysis (FY2025):**
IREN reported strong revenue for the fiscal year 2025, with total revenue
reaching **$501,023,000**. The cost of revenue was **$158,992,000**, which
implies a healthy gross profit. Selling, general, and administrative (SG&A)
expenses amounted to *
*$136,458,000**, and depreciation and amortization were **$181,136,000**. These
operating expenses led to a positive but modest operating income of *
*$17,327,000**. Despite the relatively low operating income, IREN recorded a substantial net income of **$
86,941,000**. This significant difference suggests substantial non-operating
income or benefits, which upon closer inspection of the detailed income
statement includes a large "Unrealized gain (loss) on financial instruments"
of $77,518,000 and "Gain on partial extinguishment of financial liabilities" of $
9,093,000. Basic Earnings Per Share (EPS) for the year was *
*$0.41**, and Diluted EPS was **$0.39**, indicating profitability on a per-share
basis.

**Balance Sheet Analysis (FY2025):**
As of June 30, 2025, IREN maintained a solid liquidity position with *
*$564,526,000** in cash and cash equivalents. Total current assets amounted to **$
641,173,000**, while total current liabilities were *
*$149,347,000**. This results in a strong current ratio of approximately 4.3x, suggesting the company can comfortably meet its short-term obligations. Total assets stood at **$
2,940,323,000**, with a significant portion (
approximately $1.93 billion) attributed to Property, Plant, and Equipment, Net, reflecting a capital-intensive business model. Long-term liabilities included **$
962,765,000** in convertible notes payable. Total liabilities were *
*$1,122,835,000**, and total stockholders' equity was **$1,817,488,000**. The
substantial stockholders' equity indicates a healthy financial structure with a
good proportion of assets funded by equity rather than solely relying on debt.

**Cash Flow Statement Analysis (FY2025):**
IREN generated *
*$245,886,000** in net cash from operating activities, which is a very positive sign, demonstrating the company's ability to generate cash from its core business operations. However, investing activities resulted in a significant net cash outflow of **$
-1,380,487,000**. This large outflow was primarily driven by substantial
payments for property, plant, and equipment net of hardware
prepayments ($573,456,000) and computer hardware prepayments ($799,171,000),
indicating aggressive expansion and investment in infrastructure. To counter
this, the company secured considerable funding from financing activities, with a
net cash inflow of *
*$1,294,735,000**. This was largely due to proceeds from convertible notes ($
701,211,000) and proceeds from the issuance of common
stock ($601,805,000). The combined effect of these activities led to a net increase of **$
160,134,000** in cash and cash equivalents during the fiscal year.

**2. Recent Financial Performance (Quarterly - Q1 2026 ended September 30, 2025)
**

**Income Statement Analysis (Q1 2026):**
For the first quarter of fiscal year 2026, IREN reported total revenue of *
*$240,295,000**. This quarterly revenue is a significant portion of the previous fiscal year's total revenue, suggesting strong operational performance or accelerated growth. Cost of revenue was **$
80,673,000**. SG&A expenses remained substantial at *
*$138,359,000**, exceeding the cost of revenue for the quarter. Depreciation and amortization were **$
85,226,000**. This led to an operating loss of *
*$-76,405,000**. Despite the operating loss, the company reported a remarkably high net income of **$
384,611,000**. This substantial profit is almost entirely attributable to a
massive "Unrealized gain (loss) on financial instruments" of *
*$664,993,000**, highlighting the significant impact of market value fluctuations on its financial instruments (likely digital assets or related derivatives). Basic EPS for the quarter was **$
1.42**, and Diluted EPS was **$1.08**. Traders should note the significant
reliance on unrealized gains for net income, as these can be highly volatile.

**Balance Sheet Analysis (Q1 2026):**
As of September 30, 2025, IREN's financial position has significantly
strengthened since the end of FY2025. Cash and cash equivalents surged to *
*$1,032,263,000**, a substantial increase from the prior fiscal year-end. Total current assets rose to **$
1,123,940,000**. Total assets increased significantly to *
*$4,267,374,000**, with Property, Plant, and Equipment, Net also increasing to **$
2,115,436,000**, indicating continued investment. Notably, "Financial assets" (
long-term investments) jumped to *
*$681,400,000** and "Derivative assets" (non-current) to **$314,400,000**,
largely reflecting the unrealized gains mentioned in the income statement. Total
current liabilities increased to *
*$203,745,000**, while total non-current liabilities (including convertible notes payable of **$
964,209,000**) increased to **$1,187,462,000**. Total liabilities grew to **$
1,391,207,000**. Stockholders' equity also saw a significant boost, reaching **$
2,876,167,000**. The overall increase in assets and equity, while supported by
financing, also reflects the impact of the unrealized gains.

**Cash Flow Statement Analysis (Q1 2026):**
In Q1 2026, IREN continued to generate positive cash from operating activities,
reporting *
*$142,353,000**. This is a solid performance for a single quarter, though it is lower than the full FY2025 operating cash flow. Investing activities again showed a substantial net cash outflow of **$
-280,877,000**, driven by payments for property, plant, and equipment net of
computer hardware

**3. Detailed Financial Analysis & Insights for Traders**

* **Revenue Growth and Volatility:** IREN demonstrates strong revenue generation
  capabilities, with the Q1 2026 revenue alone being nearly half of the entire
  previous fiscal year's revenue. This suggests significant operational growth
  or a favorable market environment. However, traders should be mindful of the
  potential volatility of the company's revenue stream if it is heavily
  dependent on cryptocurrency mining, as the value of mined assets can fluctuate
  significantly.
* **Profitability Driven by Non-Operating Gains:** While operating income was
  positive in FY2025, it turned into an operating loss in Q1 2026. The
  substantial net income in both periods was heavily influenced by "unrealized
  gains on financial instruments." This highlights a key risk factor: IREN's
  profitability is significantly exposed to market fluctuations of its financial
  instruments, which are likely digital assets. Traders need to understand the
  nature of these financial instruments and their associated market risks. A
  reversal of these unrealized gains could significantly impact future net
  income and stock performance.
* **Aggressive Capital Expenditure:** The consistent and large cash outflows
  from investing activities (over $1.38 billion in FY2025 and $280 million in Q1
  2026) for property, plant, equipment, and hardware prepayments indicate a
  company in a significant growth and expansion phase. This could be positive
  for long-term growth by increasing capacity and market share but also requires
  substantial ongoing funding and efficient deployment of capital.
* **Reliance on Financing Activities:** To fund its capital-intensive expansion,
  IREN has heavily relied on financing activities, particularly through
  convertible notes and common stock issuance. While this has successfully
  increased cash reserves and funded growth, it also implies potential
  shareholder dilution and increased debt obligations. Traders should monitor
  the company's ability to service its debt and the potential for further
  dilution.
* **Strong Liquidity but Watch Asset Composition:** The company has a strong
  cash position and current ratio, indicating good short-term liquidity.
  However, a significant portion of its non-current assets are property, plant,
  and equipment, and increasingly, financial and derivative assets. The value of
  these financial and derivative assets can be highly volatile, impacting the
  overall asset base and equity.
* **Operational Efficiency:** The high SG&A expenses relative to revenue,
  particularly in Q1 2026 where they exceeded the cost of revenue, warrant
  attention. While some of this might be tied to growth infrastructure or
  one-time expenses, sustained high SG&A could erode core operating
  profitability. Traders should look for trends in these expenses relative to
  revenue.
* **Convertible Debt:** The presence of significant convertible notes payable ($
  964.2 million in Q1 2026) suggests potential for future equity dilution if
  these notes are converted into common stock. Traders should consider the
  potential impact on EPS and ownership structure, especially as the conversion
  terms become more favorable for holders.
