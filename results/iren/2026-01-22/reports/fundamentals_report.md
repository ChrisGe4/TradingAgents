### Fundamental Analysis Report: Iris Energy Limited (IREN)

**Date:** January 22, 2026
**Period Analyzed:** Q1 Fiscal Year 2026 (Quarter ended September 30, 2025)

---

#### 1. Company Profile and Operations

Iris Energy Limited (IREN) is a leading owner and operator of next-generation
data centers, primarily focused on Bitcoin mining. The company emphasizes 100%
renewable energy use. Based on the latest quarterly filing, IREN is in a phase
of aggressive expansion, significantly increasing its hardware capacity and
infrastructure.

#### 2. Income Statement Analysis: The "Paper Profit" vs. Operating Reality

For the quarter ended September 30, 2025, IREN reported a complex set of
results:

* **Total Revenue:** $240.3 million.
* **Core Operating Loss:** Despite significant revenue, the company generated an
  **operating loss
  of $76.4 million**. This was driven by high **Selling, General, and Administrative (SG&A) expenses ($
  138.4 million)** and **Depreciation/Amortization ($85.2 million)**.
* **Net Income Surge:** Interestingly, the company reported a **Net Income
  of $384.6 million**. This disconnect is explained by a massive **$665 million
  unrealized gain on financial instruments**. For a Bitcoin miner, this
  typically reflects the appreciation of Bitcoin holdings or related derivatives
  on the balance sheet.
* **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** Basic EPS stood
  at $1.42, while Diluted EPS was $1.08.

**Trader's Insight:** The company's bottom line is currently a hostage to the
valuation of its digital assets. Traders should focus on "Cash Cost of
Production" rather than Net Income, as the latter is heavily skewed by non-cash
valuation adjustments.

#### 3. Balance Sheet Strength and Capital Structure

As of September 30, 2025, IREN maintains a robust but complex balance sheet:

* **Liquidity:** The company is exceptionally liquid, holding **$1.032 billion
  in cash and cash equivalents**.
* **Asset Growth:** Total assets
  reached **$4.27 billion**, with Property, Plant, and Equipment (PPE) valued at $
  2.12 billion, reflecting massive investment in mining rigs and data centers.
* **Debt Profile:** The company has **$964.2 million in Convertible Notes
  Payable**. While these are non-current liabilities, they represent a
  significant future dilution risk or repayment obligation.
* **Equity:** Total stockholders’ equity
  is **$2.88 billion**, though it is important to note that this includes an accumulated deficit of $
  211.6 million, indicating the company is not yet consistently profitable from
  core operations.

#### 4. Cash Flow and Financing: Dilution as a Funding Strategy

The cash flow statement reveals how IREN is funding its massive growth:

* **Operating Cash Flow:** Net cash from operating activities was positive
  at **$142.4 million**, showing that the business generates cash before massive non-cash depreciation and stock-based compensation ($
  72.4 million).
* **Investing Activities:** The company spent **$280.9 million** on capital
  expenditures, primarily for new computer hardware and PPE.
* **Financing Activities:** This is the most critical area. IREN raised **$618.4
  million** during the quarter through the issuance of ordinary shares via "
  at-the-market" (ATM) offerings.

**Trader's Insight:** IREN is aggressively using its equity as a "currency" to
fund expansion. While this keeps the cash balance high, it leads to significant
shareholder dilution.

#### Final Fundamental Assessment

IREN is currently a "growth-at-all-costs" player in the Bitcoin mining and
high-performance computing space. Its financial health is characterized by *
*massive cash reserves** and **strong operational cash flow**, but its GAAP
profitability is highly volatile due to crypto price fluctuations. The primary
risk for traders is the **continual dilution** from ATM offerings and the *
*large convertible debt** load. However, its $1B+ cash pile provides a
significant "war chest" to survive market downturns or acquire distressed
assets.
