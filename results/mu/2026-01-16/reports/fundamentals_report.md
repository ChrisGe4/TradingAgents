## Micron Technology (MU) - Comprehensive Fundamental Report (2011-2025)

**Date of Report:** 2026-01-16

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Micron Technology's (MU)
fundamental financial information from 2011 to 2025, derived from its annual
balance sheets, cash flow statements, and income statements. The aim is to offer
detailed insights to inform trading decisions.

---

### I. Financial Performance Overview

Micron Technology has experienced significant fluctuations in its financial
performance over the past fifteen years, characteristic of the cyclical nature
of the semiconductor industry.

**Revenue:**
Micron's revenue has shown substantial growth over the analyzed period, albeit
with considerable volatility.

* **2011-2014:** Revenue grew steadily from $8.79 billion (2011) to $16.36
  billion (2014).
* **2015-2016:** A significant drop occurred, with revenue falling
  to $16.19 billion (2015) and further to $12.40 billion (2016), reflecting a
  downturn in the semiconductor market.
* **2017-2018:** A strong recovery saw revenue more than double
  to $20.32 billion (2017) and then jump to an impressive $30.39 billion (2018).
* **2019-2020:** Another downturn led to a decline
  to $23.41 billion (2019) and further to $21.44 billion (2020).
* **2021-2022:** The company experienced a robust rebound, with revenue
  reaching $27.71 billion (2021) and peaking at $30.76 billion (2022).
* **2023-2024:** A sharp decline is evident, with revenue plummeting
  to $15.54 billion (2023) and slightly recovering to $25.11 billion (2024).
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** The most recent data shows a strong recovery,
  with revenue reaching $37.38 billion, indicating a significant upturn in the
  semiconductor market.

**Gross Profit & Margins:**
Gross profit margins have mirrored revenue trends, demonstrating the company's
sensitivity to market cycles.

* **2011-2014:** Gross margin improved from $1.76 billion (2011) to $5.44
  billion (2014), with margins ranging from approximately 20% to 33%.
* **2015-2016:** Gross profit declined significantly
  to $5.22 billion (2015) and a much lower $2.51 billion (2016), with margins
  dropping to around 32% and 20% respectively.
* **2017-2018:** A sharp increase in gross profit to $8.44 billion (2017) and $
  10.70 billion (2018), with margins improving to roughly 41% and 35%.
* **2019-2020:** Gross profit decreased to $7.38 billion (2019) and $6.55
  billion (2020), with margins around 31% and 31%.
* **2021-2022:** A strong increase to $10.42 billion (2021) and $13.90 billion (
  2022), with margins around 38% and 45%.
* **2023:** A significant negative gross profit of -$1.42 billion in 2023
  indicates severe pricing pressure and potentially high inventory write-downs.
  This is a critical point for traders, suggesting significant headwinds in that
  year.
* **2024:** A recovery to $5.61 billion in gross profit, showing improvement
  from the 2023 low.
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** A substantial recovery in gross profit to $
  14.87 billion, indicating strong market conditions and improved profitability.

**Operating Income:**
Operating income has been highly cyclical, closely tracking gross profit.

* **2011-2012:** Operating income was positive but
  modest, $755 million (2011) and -$618 million (2012).
* **2013-2014:** A significant turnaround to $236 million (2013) and $3.09
  billion (2014).
* **2015-2016:** Declined to $2.99 billion (2015) and then a severe drop to $168
  million (2016).
* **2017-2018:** Strong recovery to $5.87 billion (2017) and an impressive $7.38
  billion (2018).
* **2019-2020:** Declined to $3.00 billion (2019) and then to $1.30 billion (
  2020).
* **2021-2022:** A rebound to $6.28 billion (2021) and $9.70 billion (2022).
* **2023:** A substantial operating *loss* of -$5.75 billion, highlighting
  significant operational challenges and market difficulties.
* **2024:** A recovery to an operating income of $1.30 billion, showing positive
  operational performance returning.
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** A very strong operating income of $9.77
  billion, indicating excellent operational leverage in the current market
  upswing.

**Net Income & EPS:**
Net income and Diluted EPS have followed the same cyclical pattern as revenue
and operating income, with periods of significant losses and strong profits.

* **2011-2012:** Net income was $190 million (2011) and -$1.03 billion (2012),
  with corresponding EPS of $0 and -$1.00. This loss in 2012 is notable.
* **2013-2014:** A rebound to $1.19 billion (2013) and $3.08 billion (2014),
  with EPS of $1.00 and $2.00.
* **2015-2016:** Declined to $2.90 billion (2015) and a loss of -$275 million (
  2016), with EPS of $2.00 and $0.00.
* **2017-2018:** Strong positive net income of $5.09 billion (2017) and $14.14
  billion (2018), with EPS of $4.00 and $11.00. The 2018 figures represent a
  peak.
* **2019-2020:** Decreased to $6.36 billion (2019) and $2.71 billion (2020),
  with EPS of $5.00 and $2.37.
* **2021-2022:** A significant rebound to $5.86 billion (2021) and $8.69
  billion (2022), with EPS of $5.14 and $7.75.
* **2023:** A substantial net loss of -$5.83 billion, resulting in an EPS of -$
  5.34. This indicates a very challenging year for the company.
* **2024:** A recovery to a net income of $778 million and an EPS of $0.70.
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** A strong net income
  of $8.54 billion and an EPS of $7.59, signaling a significant return to
  profitability.

### II. Financial Position (Balance Sheet)

**Total Assets:**
Micron's total assets have steadily increased over the years, reflecting
continuous investment in its operations.

* **2011-2013:** Assets grew from $14.75 billion (2011) to $19.12 billion (
  2013).
* **2014-2016:** Continued growth to $22.50 billion (2014), $24.14 billion (
  2015), and $27.54 billion (2016).
* **2017-2019:** Significant expansion with total assets
  reaching $35.34 billion (2017), $43.38 billion (2018), and $48.89 billion (
  2019).
* **2020-2025:** Assets further increased to $53.68 billion (2020), $58.85
  billion (2021), $66.28 billion (2022), $64.25 billion (
  2023), $69.42 billion (2024), and an impressive $82.80 billion (2025). The dip
  in 2023 followed by a strong recovery and growth in 2024 and 2025 is
  noteworthy.

**Total Liabilities:**
Total liabilities have also shown an upward trend, though with some
fluctuations, reflecting increased scale and financing needs.

* **2011-2013:** Liabilities increased from $4.90 billion (2011) to $9.11
  billion (2013).
* **2014-2015:** Rose to $10.87 billion (2014) and $10.86 billion (2015).
* **2016-2018:** Increased to $14.61 billion (2016), $15.85 billion (2017), and
  a significant $10.11 billion (2018), indicating some debt reduction or
  liability management.
* **2019-2025:** Liabilities increased to $12.02 billion (2019), $14.68
  billion (2020), $14.92 billion (2021), and $16.38 billion (2022). There's a
  notable increase in liabilities in 2023 to $20.13 billion, followed by $24.28
  billion in 2024, and a further increase to $28.63 billion in 2025. This recent
  trend suggests increased reliance on debt or other obligations to finance
  growth.

**Total Equity:**
Shareholders' equity reflects the residual value after liabilities.

* **2011-2012:** Equity was $9.85 billion (2011) and $8.42 billion (2012),
  showing a decrease during a loss-making year.
* **2013-2014:** A recovery to $10.01 billion (2013) and $11.57 billion (2014).
* **2015-2016:** Increased to $13.24 billion (2015) and $12.93 billion (2016).
* **2017-2025:** Equity showed significant growth,
  reaching $19.47 billion (2017), $33.16 billion (
  2018), $36.77 billion (2019), $38.99 billion (2020), $43.93 billion (2021), $
  49.91 billion (2022), $44.12 billion (2023), $45.13 billion (2024), and $54.17
  billion (2025). The dip in 2023 corresponds with the net loss, but a strong
  rebound and continued growth in 2024 and 2025 indicate solid shareholder value
  creation.

### III. Cash Flow Analysis

**Net Cash from Operating Activities:**
Operational cash flow is crucial for sustaining and growing the business.

* **2011-2012:** Cash flow from operations was
  positive, $2.48 billion (2011) and $2.11 billion (2012), even in a year with
  net losses, indicating the strength of non-cash charges (like depreciation).
* **2013-2014:** Increased to $1.81 billion (2013) and $5.70 billion (2014).
* **2015-2016:** Rose to $5.21 billion (2015) and $3.17 billion (2016).
* **2017-2018:** Strong cash
  generation, $8.15 billion (2017) and an exceptional $17.40 billion (2018).
* **2019-2022:** Continued strong performance with $13.19 billion (2019), $8.31
  billion (2020), $12.47 billion (2021), and $15.18 billion (2022).
* **2023:** A significant drop to $1.56 billion, reflecting the challenging
  operational environment.
* **2024:** A rebound to $8.51 billion.
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** Very strong operating cash flow of $17.53
  billion, indicating excellent cash generation from core business operations.

**Capital Expenditures (Investing Activities):**
Capital expenditures represent investments in property, plant, and equipment.

* **2011-2013:** Capital expenditures were consistently high,
  at $2.55 billion (2011), $1.70 billion (2012), and $1.24 billion (2013).
* **2014-2017:** Increased to $2.66 billion (2014), $4.02 billion (
  2015), $5.82 billion (2016), and $4.73 billion (2017). These high figures
  suggest continuous investment in manufacturing capacity and technology.
* **2018-2022:** Further increased to $8.88 billion (2018), $9.78 billion (
  2019), $8.22 billion (2020), $10.03 billion (2021), and $12.07 billion (2022).
  This sustained high level of capital expenditure is typical for a
  growth-oriented semiconductor company.
* **2023:** Capital expenditures decreased to $7.68 billion, likely in response
  to the market downturn and reduced profitability.
* **2024:** Further decreased to $8.39 billion.
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** A significant increase to $15.86 billion,
  signaling aggressive expansion and investment in anticipation of future
  growth.

**Net Cash from Financing Activities:**
Financing activities include debt issuance/repayment, stock repurchases, and
dividends.

* **2011-2012:** Negative financing cash flow
  of -$1.04 billion (2011) and positive $497 million (2012).
* **2013-2016:** Positive cash flow from financing in
  2013 ($322 million) and 2014 (-$1.95 billion), then changed
  to -$718 million (2015) and positive $1.75 billion (2016).
* **2017-2022:** A mix of positive and negative cash
  flows: $349 million (2017), -$7.78 billion (2018), -$2.44 billion (2019), -$
  317 million (2020), -$1.78 billion (2021), and -$2.98 billion (2022). The
  significant negative figures in recent years (2018, 2019, 2021, 2022) are
  largely due to debt repayments and share repurchases, indicating a focus on
  strengthening the balance sheet and returning value to shareholders when
  profitable.
* **2023:** A positive cash flow of $4.98 billion, primarily driven by proceeds
  from debt issuance to counter the operational challenges.
* **2024:** A negative cash flow of -$1.84 billion, reflecting debt repayments.
* **2025 (Projected/Reported):** Negative cash flow of -$850 million, also
  indicating debt repayments and potentially dividends.

### III. Key Trends and Insights for Traders:

* **Highly Cyclical Industry:** Micron's financial performance is strongly tied
  to the semiconductor market cycles. Traders should be aware of these inherent
  boom-bust patterns. The significant swings between large profits/revenues and
  substantial losses/revenue declines are a defining characteristic.
* **Recent Recovery (2024-2025):** After a challenging year in 2023 with a net
  loss and negative gross profit, Micron has shown a remarkable recovery in 2024
  and 2025 (projected/reported). Revenue, gross profit, operating income, and
  net income have all surged, with 2025 projected to be a strong year,
  surpassing previous highs in some metrics. This suggests the company is
  currently in an upward cycle.
* **Aggressive Capital Expenditures:** Micron consistently invests heavily in
  property, plant, and equipment, indicating a long-term growth strategy and
  commitment to maintaining a competitive edge in technology and manufacturing.
  The significant increase in capex in 2025 after a dip in 2023 and 2024 points
  to confidence in future market demand.
* **Debt Management:** While liabilities have generally increased with asset
  growth, the company has also engaged in significant debt repayments during
  profitable periods. The recent increase in debt issuance in 2023 was a
  strategic move during a downturn, followed by repayments in 2024 and 2025,
  suggesting proactive financial management.
* **Shareholder Returns (Historically):** In profitable years, Micron has
  engaged in share repurchases (e.g., 2018, 2019, 2021, 2022) and paid
  dividends (e.g., 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025), indicating a commitment to returning
  value to shareholders when financial conditions allow.
* **Liquidity:** Cash and cash equivalents have generally trended upwards,
  providing a buffer during downturns and supporting strategic investments. The
  substantial increase in cash from 2024 to 2025 (from $7.04 billion to $9.64
  billion) further strengthens its liquidity position.
* **Inventory Fluctuations:** While not explicitly analyzed in depth, the "
  Increase/Decrease in Inventories" in the cash flow statement shows significant
  swings. For example, a large increase in
  2023 ($3.56 billion) followed by a decrease in 2025 (-$520 million) could
  suggest inventory build-up during a downturn and subsequent drawdown during a
  recovery, which can impact profitability and cash flow.
* **R&D Spending:** Research and Development expenses have shown a consistent
  upward trend, emphasizing the importance of innovation in the semiconductor
  industry. This sustained investment is crucial for long-term competitiveness.

**Conclusion for Traders:**
Micron Technology presents a high-growth, high-volatility opportunity. The
company has demonstrated a strong ability to recover from downturns, with recent
financial data pointing to a robust upturn in the semiconductor market cycle for
2024 and especially 2025. Traders should monitor industry-wide demand and
pricing trends for DRAM and NAND memory, as these heavily influence Micron's
performance. The company's significant capital expenditures and consistent R&D
spending are positive indicators for long-term growth. However, the cyclical
nature necessitates careful risk management. The recent substantial increase in
revenue and profitability in 2025 indicates strong current momentum.
