### Rationale

Here is why we are taking this risk. The Bear argued that the buybacks are "exit
liquidity," but frankly, that doesn't hold water. Fraudulent companies—actual
Ponzis or laundromats—hoard cash to maintain the illusion of solvency or siphon
it to offshore accounts. They do not aggressively shrink their float by
returning nearly $2 billion to the public markets in under a year. That behavior
signals management's confidence in their own books.

Furthermore, the valuation compression is massive. We are getting a company
growing earnings at 80% for a 30% discount compared to where it was 48 hours
ago. Even if the SEC probe results in a fine, or if they have to tighten data
compliance, the core "Axon" engine is clearly effective at monetizing attention.
The Bear is betting on a total implosion; the Bull is betting on business as
usual. The asymmetric bet here is that the truth is somewhere in the middle,
which still puts the stock significantly higher than $565.
