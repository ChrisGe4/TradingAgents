## NVDA Technical Analysis Report - December 25, 2025

This report provides a comprehensive technical analysis of NVIDIA (NVDA) stock,
incorporating key indicators to assess its current market condition, trend,
momentum, and volatility. The analysis covers the period from June 25, 2025, to
December 25, 2025.

### Trend Analysis

**Short-to-Medium Term Trend (50-day SMA):**
NVDA's 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) has shown a dynamic trajectory over
the past six months. Initially, in late June and July, the 50-day SMA was in an
upward trend, indicating a positive short-to-medium term sentiment. It gradually
ascended from around 130 in late June to approximately 180 by late October. This
upward movement suggests a sustained buying interest and positive price action
during this period. However, a noticeable shift occurred in early November.
After peaking around 183-187 in late October/early November, the 50-day SMA
began to exhibit a downward trajectory, indicating a weakening of the
short-to-medium term bullish trend. By late December, the 50-day SMA has
slightly declined to around 185, suggesting that the previously strong upward
momentum has stalled, and the stock is consolidating or experiencing a minor
downtrend in the short-to-medium term. The current value of 185.84 on December
24th, compared to earlier highs, suggests a slight cooling off but still holding
above earlier levels in the analyzed period.

**Long-Term Trend (200-day SMA):**
The 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) for NVDA demonstrates a consistent and
strong long-term bullish trend throughout the entire six-month period. Starting
at approximately 129 in late June, the 200-day SMA has steadily climbed to over
158 by late December. This sustained upward movement in the long-term SMA is a
strong bullish signal, indicating that despite any short-term fluctuations, NVDA
has been in a significant uptrend over an extended period. The current value of
158.71 on December 24th is notably below the current price action, which is
hovering around the 188-189 range, reinforcing the idea of a strong long-term
uptrend. This suggests that the stock is trading well above its long-term
average, which is generally a positive sign for long-term investors.

**Comparison of SMAs (Golden/Death Cross):**
Examining the relationship between the 50-day and 200-day SMAs, a "Golden Cross"
scenario (where the 50-day SMA crosses above the 200-day SMA) appears to have
occurred earlier in the year, prior to this six-month window, as both averages
were already in a strong uptrend at the beginning of this period, with the
50-day consistently above the 200-day. Throughout this period, the 50-day SMA
has consistently remained above the 200-day SMA, confirming a robust bullish
alignment in both medium and long-term trends. Even with the recent slight
downturn in the 50-day SMA, it remains comfortably above the 200-day SMA,
indicating that the long-term bullish momentum is still intact and dominant.
There is no indication of a "Death Cross" (where the 50-day SMA crosses below
the 200-day SMA) occurring or being imminent, which would signal a bearish
reversal in the long term.

### Momentum Analysis

**MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence):**
The MACD indicator reveals fluctuating momentum in NVDA. In the initial months (
July to mid-October), MACD values were largely positive, often significantly
above the zero line, indicating strong bullish momentum. There were periods of
heightened momentum, for example, a peak around 7.98 in mid-July and another
around 6.66 in early August. However, from mid-October onwards, the MACD has
shown a clear decline. It moved from positive values around 4-5 in late October
to negative territory in late November and December. The MACD value on December
24th stands at -0.37, indicating that bearish momentum has started to gain
traction in the very short term. This downward trend and crossover into negative
territory suggest that the buying pressure has significantly diminished, and
selling pressure is now more prevalent. This is a crucial signal for traders, as
it often precedes or accompanies a price correction or a change in the
short-term trend.

**RSI (Relative Strength Index):**
NVDA's RSI has experienced significant swings, signaling periods of both
overbought and approaching oversold conditions. In July, the RSI was frequently
in the overbought territory (above 70), reaching as high as 79.88 in mid-July,
indicating intense buying interest and potential for a pullback. Similarly, in
late October, the RSI again surged into the overbought zone, hitting 73.13 on
October 29th and 69.56 on November 3rd, suggesting strong upward momentum.
Following this, the RSI began a sharp decline throughout November and into
December. By early December, the RSI touched 41.53 on November 25th and 37.15 on
December 17th, approaching the oversold threshold (30). The current RSI on
December 24th is 56.89. While not in overbought or oversold territory, the
journey from overbought in October/November to the mid-50s and even into the 30s
in December indicates a substantial loss of bullish momentum and an increase in
selling pressure. The current level suggests a relatively neutral momentum, but
the recent trajectory points to a cooling off from previous highs and a
potential for further declines if selling pressure intensifies.

### Volatility Analysis

**ATR (Average True Range):**
The Average True Range (ATR) for NVDA has shown an interesting pattern,
indicating periods of increasing and decreasing volatility. In late June and
early July, the ATR was relatively low, around 3.8-4.0, suggesting less volatile
price movements. However, as the stock price started to rally, the ATR gradually
increased, reaching around 7.5 in mid-November. This increase in ATR suggests
that the magnitude of daily price swings expanded as the stock trended upwards
and then began its short-term correction. By late December, the ATR is around
5.79, slightly lower than its November highs but still elevated compared to the
June/July levels. The higher ATR values in the latter part of the period (
November-December) suggest that NVDA has experienced more significant price
fluctuations, implying increased risk and potential for larger price movements
in either direction. Traders should be aware of this elevated volatility when
setting stop-loss orders or determining position sizes.

**Bollinger Bands (Upper and Lower Bands):**
Bollinger Bands have provided valuable insights into NVDA's price extremes and
volatility. In July and August, the price often tested or traded along the upper
Bollinger Band, particularly when the stock was in a strong uptrend and the RSI
was in overbought territory. This indicates strong bullish pressure, with prices
pushing the upper limits of their typical range. In late October, similar
behavior was observed, with the price breaking above the upper band on October
28th (close 201.02 vs UB 196.29) and October 29th (close 207.03 vs UB 200.94),
signaling extreme bullishness and potentially unsustainable price extensions.
However, as momentum shifted in November and December, the price began to
retreat from the upper band. In December, the price has primarily traded within
the bands, and on several occasions, it approached the lower Bollinger Band,
notably on December 17th (close 170.94 vs LB 172.61), where the price briefly
traded below the lower band, indicating a potential oversold condition in the
very short term. The current upper band on December 24th is 190.19 and the lower
band is 171.38, indicating a relatively wide range, reflecting the increased
volatility seen in the ATR. The current price of 188.61 is close to the upper
band, suggesting a recent upward movement within the bands, but also hinting
that it could be approaching a resistance level or an overbought zone on a
short-term basis.

### Volume-Weighted Moving Average (VWMA)

**VWMA:**
The Volume-Weighted Moving Average (VWMA) integrates volume data into the moving
average calculation, providing a more accurate representation of the average
price where most of the trading activity occurred. NVDA's VWMA has largely
mirrored the price action and the other moving averages. It was in a strong
uptrend from July to late October, moving from around 148 to over 190. This
suggests that the upward price movement during this period was supported by
significant trading volume, lending credibility to the trend. However, from
early November, the VWMA also started to decline, moving from highs around 196
to its current level of 180.21 on December 24th. The fact that the current
price (188.61) is above the VWMA (180.21) could be interpreted in two ways:
either the price has recently gained strength and is trading above its
volume-weighted average, or it could be a sign that the recent upward move is
not fully supported by strong volume, and therefore could be less sustainable.
Given the recent MACD and RSI decline, it might suggest that the current price
strength needs to be confirmed with increasing volume to sustain itself.

### Conclusion

NVDA exhibits a **strong long-term bullish trend** as evidenced by the
consistently rising 200-day SMA and the 50-day SMA remaining above it. However,
the **short-to-medium term trend has shown signs of weakening**, with the 50-day
SMA exhibiting a slight downward trend in recent weeks.

Momentum indicators **(MACD and RSI) point towards a loss of bullish momentum**
and an increase in selling pressure in the short term. The MACD has crossed into
negative territory, and the RSI has retreated from overbought levels, even
touching near oversold conditions recently, before bouncing back to a neutral
zone.

Volatility, as measured by **ATR and Bollinger Bands, has increased**,
suggesting larger daily price swings and a more dynamic trading environment. The
price has been trading within a wider Bollinger Band range, occasionally testing
the lower band, indicating increased corrective phases.

The **VWMA generally supports the price trends**, showing an uptrend when volume
was strong and a subsequent decline as momentum waned. The current price is
above the VWMA, but this needs to be observed for sustained volume support.

**Overall, while the long-term outlook for NVDA remains bullish, the
short-to-medium term indicates a period of consolidation, potential correction,
and weakening momentum.** Traders should exercise caution, closely monitor
momentum indicators for potential reversals, and consider the increased
volatility when planning trades.
