Palantir Technologies (PLTR) has demonstrated a significant positive shift in
its financial performance over the past five years, from 2020 to 2024,
transitioning from substantial losses to consistent profitability and robust
cash generation from operations. This comprehensive report details the key
aspects of the company's financial health, drawing insights from its income
statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.

**Income Statement Analysis:**

Palantir's revenue has shown remarkable growth year-over-year, indicating
increasing demand for its data analytics platforms. Starting
from $1.09 billion in 2020, revenue climbed steadily to $1.54 billion in
2021, $1.91 billion in 2022, $2.23 billion in 2023, and reached $2.87 billion in
2024. This consistent top-line expansion is a strong positive signal for
traders, suggesting a growing market presence and successful customer
acquisition.

The cost of revenue has naturally increased in line with rising revenue, but the
company has managed to maintain or improve its gross profit margins. Gross
profit surged from $740.1 million in 2020 to an impressive $2.30 billion in
2024. This trend indicates efficient management of the direct costs associated
with delivering its services, contributing to a healthy gross margin.

Operating expenses, while increasing in absolute terms over the period, show a
more nuanced trend. After a high
of $1.91 billion in 2020, operating expenses saw a slight dip to $1.61 billion
in 2021 before steadily rising
to $1.99 billion in 2024. The crucial insight here is the company's ability to achieve profitability despite these expenses. The most significant turnaround is observed in operating income (loss), which moved from a substantial loss of $
1.17 billion in 2020 to a positive operating income
of $119.9 million in 2023 and further to $310.4 million in 2024. This transition
from operating losses to profits is a strong indicator of improved operational
efficiency and scalability.

Net income (loss) mirrors the operating income trend, marking a pivotal shift
for the company. After suffering significant net losses
of $1.17 billion in 2020, $520.4 million in 2021,
and $371.1 million in 2022, Palantir achieved profitability with a net income of $
217.4 million in 2023 and $467.9 million in 2024. This sustained profitability
is a critical development for investor confidence. Consequently, earnings per
share (EPS), both basic and diluted, turned positive in 2023 and continued to
rise in 2024, reflecting the company's enhanced earning power for its common
stockholders.

**Balance Sheet Analysis:**

Palantir's balance sheet reflects a strengthening financial position. Cash and
cash equivalents have fluctuated, notably decreasing
from $2.60 billion in 2022 to $831.0 million in 2023 before rebounding to $2.10
billion in 2024. This fluctuation is largely explained by the company's
investing activities.

A significant trend on the asset side is the substantial increase in marketable
securities. From no reported marketable securities in 2020, the value grew
to $234.2 million in 2021, dipped to $35.1 million in 2022, but then exploded
to $2.84 billion in 2023 and $3.13 billion in 2024. This indicates Palantir is
actively investing its cash reserves in highly liquid assets.

Accounts receivable have consistently increased from $156.9 million in 2020 to $
575.0 million in 2024, which is generally consistent with its growing revenue.
Traders should monitor the accounts receivable turnover ratio to ensure
efficient collection practices. Total current assets and total assets have shown
robust growth, with total assets expanding from $2.69 billion in 2020 to $6.34
billion in 2024, largely driven by the increases in cash, marketable securities,
and accounts receivable.

On the liabilities side, total current liabilities have generally increased,
from $603.8 million in 2020 to $996.0 million in 2024, with a slight dip in
2022. Total liabilities also show an increasing trend,
from $1.17 billion in 2020 to $1.25 billion in 2024, but this growth is slower
than that of total assets. This implies a strengthening capital structure.

A key positive development is the significant improvement in total stockholders'
equity, which grew from $1.52 billion in 2020 to $5.00 billion in 2024. This
sustained growth in equity underscores the company's increasing financial
stability and retained earnings from its recent profitability.

**Cash Flow Statement Analysis:**

The cash flow statement highlights Palantir's impressive ability to generate
cash from its core operations. Net cash provided by operating activities has
seen a dramatic turnaround and consistent growth, moving from a
negative $296.6 million in 2020 to a strong positive $333.9 million in 2021, and
further to $712.2 million in 2023 and $1.15 billion in 2024. This increasing
operating cash flow is a very healthy sign, indicating that the company's
business model is generating substantial free cash, reducing its reliance on
external financing for day-to-day operations.

Net cash used in investing activities has shown significant outflows,
particularly in 2023 and 2024. This is primarily attributed to substantial
purchases of marketable securities ($5.64 billion in 2023 and $5.40 billion in
2024), partly offset by proceeds from sales and maturities of these
securities ($2.89 billion in 2023 and $5.07 billion in 2024). These activities
explain the fluctuations in cash and marketable securities on the balance sheet
and suggest an active treasury management strategy. While these are outflows,
they represent investments rather than operational burn.

Net cash provided by financing activities has varied, with a large inflow
of $1.04 billion in 2020 primarily due to proceeds from common stock issuance and debt. In subsequent years, contributions from the exercise of common stock options have been a consistent positive factor, alongside some share repurchases and tax-related payments for equity awards. The financing activities in 2024 provided $
463.4 million, indicating continued access to capital and management of equity.

The net increase (decrease) in cash, cash equivalents, and restricted cash
generally reflects the interplay of these activities. A notable decrease of $
1.78 billion occurred in 2023, but this was largely due to the aforementioned
investments in marketable securities, which are still liquid assets. The overall
trend indicates a strong liquidity position.

**Comprehensive Summary for Traders:**

Palantir Technologies has undergone a significant transformation, evolving into
a profitable and cash-generative enterprise. The consistent revenue growth,
coupled with a successful shift from net losses to substantial net income,
demonstrates strong business fundamentals and effective operational management.
The robust and growing operating cash flow provides the company with significant
financial flexibility. The strategic deployment of cash into marketable
securities indicates a prudent approach to treasury management, ensuring
liquidity while aiming for returns. While substantial investments in marketable
securities have led to fluctuations in the "Cash and Cash Equivalents" line, the
overall liquidity and asset base remain strong.
