### The Decision

**Recommendation: BUY (Strategic Accumulation)**

I am authorizing the **BUY** recommendation, but I am **rejecting the Trader's
aggressive 40% upfront allocation.**

The Risky Analyst is correct on the **Business Value** (profitability
inflection, margin expansion), but the Safe Analyst is correct on the **Price
Dynamics** (supply overhang from dilution, broken technicals). The Trader’s
original plan to deploy 40% immediately is reckless given the likelihood of a
test of the 200-day Moving Average. We will adopt the Neutral Analyst’s "Pilot
Position" approach to secure an entry without exposing nearly half the capital
to an immediate 10-13% drawdown risk.

### 2. Rationale for the Decision

My decision rests on the distinction between **Solvency** and **Liquidity**.
SoFi is solvent and growing (Bull case), but the stock is currently suffering a
liquidity shock due to the massive share issuance (Bear case).

* **Why we are buying:** I agree with the Trader and Bull Analyst that *"the
  market is currently pricing in fear of the secondary offering, not a
  deterioration of the business."* The "Financial Services Productivity Loop"
  and the shift to capital-light revenue justify a higher multiple than a legacy
  bank.
* **Why we are waiting to size up:** The Safe Analyst’s warning is the most
  critical risk management input: *"In my world, we don't fly jets when the
  sensors tell us the engines are failing."* The stock is structurally broken in
  the short term. The $1.7B supply overhang acts as a ceiling. As the Neutral
  Analyst noted, *"Dilution is a mathematical headwind... ignoring the supply
  overhang is how traders get trapped."*

We will not pay a premium for impatience. We will let the sellers come to us.

### 4. Reflection on Decision Making

**Lesson Applied: Avoid "Anchoring Bias" regarding Price vs. Market Cap.**
A potential error in this trade is looking at the
price ($25) and thinking it is "cheap" because it used to be $30. This is a
mistake. Because the share count increased by 15%, the *Market Cap*
at $25 today is higher than the Market Cap was at $25 last month.

* **Correction:** We are respecting the dilution. We are not aggressively buying
  the current dip (Step 1 reduced to 20%) because the shares are mathematically
  worth less than they were pre-dilution. We demand a deeper discount (Step 2)
  to offset the dilution penalty.

**Final Order:** Execute the **BUY** with the **20/50/30** split structure
immediately.
