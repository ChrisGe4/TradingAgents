### My Decision

**Recommendation: BUY (Aggressive Accumulation)**

I am siding with the **Bull Analyst**, but with a heavy dose of respect for the
Bear’s technical warnings.

While the Bear makes a valid point about short-term supply overhang and the
optical ugliness of the dilution, their valuation argument is fundamentally
flawed. You cannot evaluate a company growing revenue at 40% with the same
multiple as a bank growing at 3%. The "PEG ratio" (Price/Earnings-to-Growth)
suggests SoFi is actually undervalued relative to its velocity. The market is
currently pricing in fear of the secondary offering, not a deterioration of the
business.

We are buying the business, not just the chart.

### The Investment Plan

entry, but we will be tactical to avoid catching the "falling knife" the Bear
warned about.

I’ve learned from general market history that when a company hits a "
profitability inflection point" (going from burning cash to rapid GAAP
profitability), the stock price eventually follows the earnings, even if it lags
temporarily due to technical noise.

* **The Dilution is digested:** The fear of the $1.7B raise is now known
  information. It’s in the price.
* **The Moat:** The Bull’s point about the "Financial Services Productivity
  Loop" is the differentiator. SoFi lowers its cost of capital through
  deposits (like a bank) but scales via software (like a tech firm). That margin
  profile (82.5%) is the winning metric here.

#### 2. Strategic Actions

We are not going "all in" today. We are going to use the Bear’s technical
targets to our advantage.

* **Step 1: The Initial Tranche (40% of position size):** Execute a Buy order at
  current market prices ($25.00 - $26.00 range). We need skin in the game in
  case the reversal happens sooner than the charts suggest.
* **Step 2: The "Bear Trap" Limits (40% of position size):** Place limit buy
  orders layered between **$22.50 and $23.50**. The Bear is likely right that
  the stock will test the 200-day SMA area. If it flushes to that level, we want
  to be liquidity providers, not panic sellers.
* **Step 3: The Confirmation Add (20% of position size):** We hold the final 20%
  in cash. We only deploy this if/when the stock reclaims the **50-day SMA (
  approx $27.50)**. This confirms the trend has reversed and validates the Bull

We must respect the Bear’s macro warning. If the "trade fragmentation" or tariff
situation causes a systemic banking sell-off:

* **Hard Stop:** Close the position if the stock closes below **$21.00** on high
