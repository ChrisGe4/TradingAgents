### Technical Analysis Report: SOFI (SoFi Technologies, Inc.)

**Date:** January 22, 2026
**Current Price (as of 2026-01-21):** $25.64

#### **Executive Summary**

SOFI is currently navigating a significant corrective phase following a powerful
bull run in late 2025. While the long-term structural uptrend remains intact,
the stock has lost its medium-term momentum and is currently testing critical
short-term support levels. Traders should exercise caution as the stock is
pinned between a rising long-term floor and a descending medium-term ceiling.

---

#### **Trend and Momentum Analysis**

* **Long-Term Bullish Foundation:** The **200-day SMA ($22.17)** continues to
  climb steadily. This indicates that despite recent volatility, the primary
  trend over the last year remains positive. The price is currently about 15%
  above this "ultimate" support line.
* **Medium-Term Bearish Shift:** The stock broke below its **50-day
  SMA ($27.46)** in early January and has failed to reclaim it. This transition from "support" to "resistance" is a classic sign of a trend shift. As long as SOFI remains below $
  27.46, the path of least resistance for the medium term is sideways to down.
* **Short-Term Weakness:** The **10-day EMA ($26.41)** is sloping downward and
  currently sits above the price. This "moving ceiling" has been suppressing
  attempts to rally throughout mid-January.
* **Negative Momentum (MACD):** The **MACD (-0.386)** is deep in negative
  territory. The lack of a "bullish cross" indicates that sellers still have the
  upper hand and no immediate reversal is signaled by momentum alone.
* **Relative Strength (RSI):** At **42.3**, the RSI reflects a "cooling off"
  period. It is not yet oversold (below 30), suggesting there is still room for
  further downside before the stock becomes technically "cheap" enough to
  trigger a reflexive bounce.

#### **Volatility and Volume Insights**

* **Bollinger Band Compression:** The price is currently hugging the **Lower
  Bollinger
  Band ($25.29)**. This level is a crucial "line in the sand." Historically, price action near the lower band can lead to a "squeeze" or a bounce, but a decisive close below it would likely trigger a stop-loss cascade down to the $
  22.00–$23.00 range.
* **Volume Weighting (VWMA):** The **
  VWMA ($27.01)** is currently higher than the spot price. This signifies that the "average" buyer over the recent period is currently underwater (at a loss). This creates "overhead supply," as these investors may look to sell and "break even" if the price rallies back toward $
  27.
