After weighing the evidence and checking my own biases, I have made a decision.

**My Recommendation: BUY**

We are going long on Meta Platforms, but we are going to be tactical about it.

**Rationale**

Here is why the Bull wins out, despite the Bear’s terrifyingly accurate
technical analysis.

The Bear’s strongest argument—that Meta is "burning cash on an unproven
moonshot"—echoes the exact sentiment the market held during Meta's 2022
collapse. I made the mistake back then of looking at the capital expenditure (
CapEx) as a liability rather than a competitive moat. I learned the hard way
that when a company generates over $90 billion in cash annually, high CapEx
isn't a death sentence; it's a barrier to entry for everyone else.

The crucial difference between the Metaverse pivot and this AI pivot is
applicability. The Metaverse was a solution looking for a problem. AI, however,
directly feeds Meta’s core engine: better ad targeting, automated content
creation, and higher engagement on Reels. The Bull is right; the infrastructure
they are building improves the product they are selling *today*, not just ten
years from now.

While the Bear is correct that the technicals are ugly—that death cross is
real—fundamental value eventually overrides technical sentiment. The market is
pricing Meta as if it’s entering a crisis, but the financials show a company
printing money. The "AI bubble" fears are valid, but if there is a bubble, I
want to own the company selling the pickaxes (or in this case, the data centers
and distribution networks), provided they can afford to build them. Meta can.

**Strategic Actions**

We are not going to dump our entire position in at once, because the Bear is
right about the immediate downward momentum. We don’t catch a falling knife; we
let it stick in the floor first.

1. **Tranche Entry:** We will split our total intended capital allocation into
   three parts. We execute the first buy immediately to establish the position.
2. **Wait for the Floor:** We will set limit orders for the second tranche at
   the next major support level, likely around the $580-$600 range, anticipating
   that the bearish technicals will drive the price slightly lower before it
   stabilizes.
3. **Hedge the Regulatory Risk:** To address the Bear’s valid concerns about
   regulatory headwinds (the under-16 ban), we will use a portion of the
   portfolio to purchase protective puts or hold a cash buffer. This protects us
   if a specific legislative hammer drops in the EU or US.
4. **Monitor ROIC:** This is the key metric. We are ignoring the daily stock
   price noise. Instead, next quarter, we look strictly at ad revenue growth
   relative to CapEx. If the AI spend isn't translating to higher ad efficiency,
   we re-evaluate.

In the past, I’ve let short-term chart damage scare me out of long-term
compounders. I won't let that happen here. The chart says sell, but the business
says buy. We follow the business.
