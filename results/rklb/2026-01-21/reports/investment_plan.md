### The Decision: Why We Are Selling

The Bull makes a seductive case about the "future economy" and "disruptive
innovation." I get it. The revenue growth is real, and the backlog is
impressive. But we are investors, not venture capitalists. We need to look at
the risk-to-reward ratio *right now*, at a price of ~$89 per share.

The Bull argues that widening losses are just "investments." I disagree. When
revenue doubles but net losses *widen*
to -$190 million, that suggests the core business model hasn't yet proven its unit economics. You can't pay rent with "accumulated deficit," and you can't justify an $
89 stock price on negative operating cash flows indefinitely.

The Bear’s point about "Neutron Euphoria" is the nail in the coffin. We are
seeing a technical "Golden Cross" and a massive run-up (550% returns). In my
experience, when a stock goes vertical based on a product (Neutron) that hasn't
fully launched yet, the market has priced in perfection. Any delay, any test
failure, or any macro shift will cause this stock to crater. The "Sell
Territory" rating downgrade is a smoke signal we cannot ignore.

We are taking the profit. We are getting off this rocket before gravity kicks
in.

I am not making that mistake again. We take the win, we respect the valuation
discipline, and we sell.
