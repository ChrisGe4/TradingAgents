### My Decision: BUY

I am siding with the Bull, but with a highly specific execution strategy that
respects the Bear’s valid technical warnings.

### The Rationale

I am making this call based on one metric that trumps everything else in this
debate: **Free Cash Flow (FCF).**

In my past experiences—specifically looking back at when I sold big tech names
during regulatory scares or M&A confusion—my biggest mistake was ignoring the
cash register. I let the headlines (or the charts) scare me out of businesses
that were printing money.

The Bear is right that the chart looks ugly *right now*. But the Bull’s point
that FCF swung from negative to nearly **$7 billion** is the signal that
matters. When a company dominates its sector and generates that kind of cash,
the downside is mathematically limited because they can fund their own growth
and buy back their own stock. The market is pricing Netflix as if the WBD deal
is going to be a disaster. If the deal doesn't happen, the stock rips higher. If
it does happen, Netflix secures an IP library that cements its dominance for a
decade.

The Bear’s argument about YouTube is noted, but Netflix competes in premium,
long-form storytelling, where they are still the undisputed king. The
valuation (PEG of 1.6) is reasonable for this level of cash generation. We are
buying the business, not the chart.

### The Investment Plan

However, we are not going to be reckless. The Bear is right that catching a
falling knife is dangerous. Here is how we play this:

**1. The "Tranche" Entry Strategy**
We are not going "all in" today. We will split our intended position into three
equal parts.

* **Tranche 1 (33%): Buy Now.** We establish a foothold to capture the long-term
  value.
* **Tranche 2 (33%): The Technical Floor.** We place limit orders near the lower
  Bollinger Band or the next major support level (likely around that $120-$125
  area mentioned in support/resistance zones). If the Bear is right and it dips
  further, we lower our cost basis.
* **Tranche 3 (33%): The Confirmation.** We hold the final third in cash until
  we see the price reclaim the **50-day SMA**. This satisfies the technical
  discipline—we add to the winner only when the trend actually reverses.

**2. The "Deal-Breaker" Stop**
We need to monitor the WBD news. If a bidding war with Paramount escalates and
Netflix offers a price that fundamentally alters their debt-to-equity ratio
beyond acceptable limits, we pause or trim. We want the assets, but not at *any*
price.

**3. Ignore the Noise**
We are looking at a 12-24 month horizon. In two years, the RSI being 30 today
won't matter. The $7 billion in cash flow will.

Let’s get to work. Execute Tranche 1 immediately.
