## Comprehensive Fundamental Report: Netflix Inc (NFLX) - As of 2025-12-25

the past week, incorporating insights from its company profile, basic
financials, and historical financial statements (Income Statement, Balance
Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement) up to the fiscal year 2024. The aim is to offer
traders a comprehensive view of the company's financial health and strategic
direction.

### 1. Company Profile

Netflix Inc. (NFLX) is a prominent American streaming service and production
company, headquartered at 121 Albright Way, Los Gatos, California, USA. It
operates within the **Communication Services** sector and the **Entertainment**
industry. Since its inception, Netflix has revolutionized the entertainment
landscape with its subscription-based model, providing a vast library of films
and television series, including critically acclaimed original content. The
company is actively focused on expanding its global presence through strategic
investments in content and innovative distribution methods to fuel subscriber
growth and redefine media consumption. Its official website
is [https://www.netflix.com](https://www.netflix.com).

### 3. Company Financial History (Annual Data)

#### 3.1. Income Statement Analysis (2021-2024)

Netflix's income statement reveals a dynamic picture of accelerating growth and
improving profitability.

* **Revenue Growth:** Total Revenue has shown consistent upward momentum, with a
  remarkable acceleration in 2024.
  From $29.69 billion in 2021, revenue grew to $31.61 billion in 2022 (a 6.4%
  year-over-year increase), then
  to $33.72 billion in 2023 (a 6.7% increase), culminating in a significant jump to **$
  39.00 billion in 2024** (a 15.6% increase). This robust growth, particularly
  the substantial increase in 2024, signals strong market penetration and
  effective content and monetization strategies. This accelerating revenue trend
  is a highly positive indicator for traders, showing increasing demand for
  Netflix's services.
* **Gross Profit Expansion:** Gross Profit has also expanded significantly,
  rising from $12.36 billion in 2021 to **$17.96 billion in 2024**. The growth
  in gross profit has outpaced the growth in cost of revenue in recent years,
  especially in 2023 and 2024, suggesting improving content production
  efficiency and effective pricing strategies.
* **Operating Expense Management:** Operating expenses (Research & Development (
  R&D) and Selling, General & Administrative (SG&A)) have steadily increased,
  reflecting ongoing investments in content creation, technology, and marketing.
  R&D grew notably to $2.92 billion in 2024 (from $2.67 billion in 2023),
  demonstrating Netflix's commitment to innovation and platform enhancement.
  While expenses are rising, the significantly higher revenue growth in 2024
  indicates the company is effectively leveraging its expenditures for top-line
  expansion.
* **Operating and Net Income Recovery and Growth:** After a slight dip in
  operating income in 2022 ($5.63 billion from $6.19 billion in 2021), the
  company staged a strong recovery,
  reaching $6.95 billion in 2023 and an impressive **$10.41 billion in 2024**.
  This strong rebound in operating income demonstrates improved operational
  efficiency and a healthier core business performance. Net Income for common
  stockholders followed a similar trajectory, decreasing
  to $4.49 billion in 2022 from $5.11 billion in 2021, then recovering
  to $5.40 billion in 2023 and surging to **$8.71 billion in 2024**. This
  substantial increase in net income is a strong positive for investors,
  directly translating to improved shareholder value and a stronger earnings
  outlook.
* **EPS Growth:** Diluted EPS has mirrored the net income trend, with a notable
  increase from $1.124 in 2021 to **$1.983 in 2024**, indicating higher
  profitability per share, partly aided by effective share buyback programs (as
  detailed in the cash flow statement).

#### 3.2. Balance Sheet Analysis (2020-2024)

Netflix's balance sheet reflects a growing asset base and disciplined financial
management, supporting its expansion efforts.

* **Asset Growth:** Total Assets have consistently grown,
  from $44.58 billion in 2020 to **$53.63 billion in 2023**, showcasing the
  company's ongoing expansion and strategic investments.
    * **Current Assets:** Current assets, including cash and short-term
      investments, have seen a significant increase, particularly in
      2023 ($13.10 billion from $9.91 billion in 2022). **Cash and Cash
      Equivalents**, after a dip in 2021, have steadily increased
      to **$7.80 billion in 2023**, providing ample liquidity for operations and strategic initiatives. A notable increase in "Other Short Term Investments" to $
      1.77 billion in 2023 from $20.97 million in 2022 suggests active and
      effective short-term capital management.
    * **Non-Current Assets:** Non-current assets are primarily comprised of **"
      Goodwill and Other Intangible Assets,"** which largely represent content
      assets crucial for Netflix's business model. This category has grown
      steadily from $30.91 billion in 2020 to **$32.45 billion in 2023**,
      indicating continuous and significant investment in intellectual property
      and original content. Net Property, Plant & Equipment (PPE) also saw
      consistent growth, reflecting ongoing infrastructure development.
* **Liability Management:** Total Liabilities have remained relatively stable
  over the period, demonstrating controlled leverage.
    * **Current Liabilities:** While total current liabilities increased in 2022
      and 2023, largely due to "Other Current Liabilities" and "Current Deferred
      Liabilities," a noteworthy point is the increase in **"Current Debt and
      Capital Lease Obligation" to $1.78 billion in 2023** from $399 million in
      2022. While this represents a short-term debt obligation, it should be
      viewed in the context of the company's overall strong liquidity and cash
      flow.
    * **Non-Current Liabilities:** **Long-term debt has shown a positive
      decreasing trend**, falling from $14.69 billion in 2020 to **$13.79
      billion in 2023**, indicating proactive debt reduction and a strengthening
      financial position, which reduces long-term financial risk for the
      company.
* **Equity Strength:** Common Stock Equity has significantly strengthened,
  rising from $15.84 billion in 2020 to **$24.74 billion in 2023**. This
  increase is primarily driven by consistent growth in **Retained Earnings**,
  which surged from $12.68 billion in 2020 to **$31.30 billion in 2023**,
  demonstrating Netflix's strong ability to retain and reinvest profits. The
  substantial increase in **Treasury Stock** to $13.17 billion in 2023
  highlights Netflix's aggressive share buyback programs, which enhance
  shareholder value by reducing the number of outstanding shares and boosting
  EPS.

#### 3.3. Cash Flow Statement Analysis (2021-2024)

Netflix's cash flow statement highlights a dramatic improvement in cash
generation and a clear, shareholder-focused capital allocation strategy.

* **Operating Cash Flow (OCF) Surge:** Operating Cash Flow has dramatically
  improved from a mere $392 million in 2021 to a robust $7.27 billion in 2023
  and sustained at **$7.36 billion in 2024**. This massive increase in OCF
  signifies that Netflix's core operations are generating substantial cash,
  significantly reducing its reliance on external financing and providing ample
  resources for investment and debt servicing. This sustained high level of OCF
  is a very strong positive for traders, indicating operational health and
  long-term sustainability.
* **Investing Activities:** Investing Cash Flow has fluctuated as the company
  makes strategic capital allocation decisions. After being negative in
  2021 (-$1.33 billion) and 2022 (-$2.07 billion) due to capital expenditures
  and business acquisitions, it turned positive in
  2023 ($541 million), largely due to significant sales of investments. In 2024, it returned to negative (-$
  2.18 billion), driven by substantial investment purchases. This pattern
  suggests strategic and opportunistic investments in content and other
  growth-oriented assets, which are essential for Netflix's future market
  leadership.
* **Financing Activities (Shareholder-Focused):** Financing Cash Flow has been
  consistently negative, indicating that Netflix is primarily using its
  generated cash to reduce debt and return capital to shareholders. Significant
  **share repurchases of -$6.04 billion in 2023 and -$6.26 billion in 2024**
  demonstrate a strong commitment to enhancing shareholder value through reduced
  share count and increased EPS. While there was a net issuance of debt of $1.39
  billion in 2024, this should be considered in the context of overall strong
  cash generation and debt reduction in prior periods, potentially signaling
  opportunistic financing for strategic investments.
* **Free Cash Flow (FCF) Transformation:** Perhaps the most compelling takeaway
  for traders is the remarkable turnaround and sustained strength in Free Cash
  Flow. From being negative in
  2021 (-$131 million), FCF became significantly positive at $1.61 billion in
  2022 and then surged to **$6.92 billion in both 2023 and 2024**. This
  consistent and high level of FCF is a critical indicator of financial health
  and operational efficiency, enabling Netflix to self-fund its growth
  initiatives, reduce debt, and execute substantial share buybacks, making it a
  highly attractive prospect for investors seeking financial stability and
  growth potential.

* **Shareholder Value Enhancement:** Consistent negative financing cash flows,

* **Strategic Content Investment:** Ongoing, substantial investments in
  intangible assets (content) and strategic purchases reflect a long-term vision

  view these investments as crucial for sustainable future growth.
