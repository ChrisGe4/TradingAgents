NFLX is currently exhibiting a strong bearish trend across multiple technical
indicators, suggesting a period of significant selling pressure and downside
momentum.

**Trend Analysis:**
The stock's price is trading significantly below both its 50-day Simple Moving
Average (SMA) and 200-day SMA. The 50-day SMA, currently at 106.59 as of
December 24, 2025, is on a clear downward trajectory, confirming a strong
bearish trend in the medium term. The price's consistent positioning below this
average indicates that the recent downward price action is persistent.
Similarly, the 200-day SMA, which is relatively flat around 113.36, serves as a
long-term benchmark. The substantial gap between the current price (93.64 on
December 24, 2025) and the 200-day SMA underscores the severity of the long-term
decline, even if the long-term average itself isn't steeply declining. This
suggests that NFLX has decisively broken below its long-term support, entering a
robust downtrend.

**Momentum Analysis:**
The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) indicator presents a nuanced
picture. On December 24, 2025, the MACD line (-4.16) crossed above its signal
line (-4.28), which is traditionally considered a short-term bullish signal,
implying a potential easing of bearish momentum or a minor upward shift.
However, it is crucial to note that both the MACD and the MACD Signal are deeply
in negative territory. This suggests that while there might be a very short-term
positive divergence or reduction in the rate of decline, the overall underlying
momentum remains strongly bearish. This crossover alone is not indicative of a
major trend reversal and should be viewed with skepticism, especially when
juxtaposed with the broader bearish trend.

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is another key momentum oscillator. For an
extended period leading up to December 24, 2025, the RSI has been consistently
hovering in the low 30s (e.g., 33.01 on Dec 24, 2025; 32.53 on Dec 23, 2025).
While an RSI below 30 typically signals an oversold condition and potential for
a rebound, a prolonged stay in the 30-40 range without a significant price
recovery often indicates strong underlying selling pressure. This extended
period of low RSI suggests that the bearish sentiment is deeply entrenched, and
buyers are reluctant to step in forcefully.

**Volatility Analysis:**
Bollinger Bands highlight that the price action is predominantly hugging the
lower Bollinger Band, with the lower band at 86.55 and the upper band at 110.15
on December 24, 2025. This positioning reinforces the bearish trend, as prices
tend to ride the bands in strong trending markets. The relatively wide spread
between the upper and lower bands suggests that the stock has experienced and
continues to exhibit considerable price volatility. The Average True Range (
ATR), which measures market volatility, shows values around 2.88 on December 24,
2025, with a slight decrease from earlier in December (e.g., 3.16 on Dec 19,
2025). This minor reduction in ATR might suggest a slight contraction in the
daily price range, but the overall volatility remains notable.

**Conclusion:**
In conclusion, NFLX is entrenched in a robust downtrend. The price action far
below both its short-term and long-term moving averages, coupled with a
downward-sloping 50-day SMA, paints a clear picture of sustained bearish
pressure. While the MACD has shown a recent bullish crossover, its deep negative
values and the persistent low RSI indicate that this is likely a minor
fluctuation within a dominant bearish trend rather than a significant reversal.
The price consistently trading near the lower Bollinger Band further confirms
the strength of the current downtrend, with volatility remaining a factor. Given
these technical observations, the immediate outlook for NFLX is overwhelmingly
bearish.
