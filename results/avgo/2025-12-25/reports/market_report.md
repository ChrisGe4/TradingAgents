  to flatten slightly, and more importantly, the price has dipped below it in

      suggests that the intense buying pressure has abated, and the stock is no
      longer enjoying strong upward momentum, potentially indicating
      profit-taking or a temporary exhaustion of buyers.

**4. Volatility Analysis**

* **Bollinger Bands (boll_ub, boll_lb):** The Bollinger Bands delineate price
  extremes relative to a moving average, providing a visual gauge of volatility.
    * During strong trending phases (e.g., September and early December), AVGO's
      price frequently "rode" the upper Bollinger Band, which is characteristic
      of robust bullish movements.
    * Conversely, dips towards the lower band (e.g., late October/early November
      and mid-December) often coincided with short-term support or potential
      buying opportunities before a rebound.
    * A significant observation is the noticeable widening of the Bollinger
      Bands from late November onwards (upper band moving from ~350 to ~420, and
      lower band from ~320 to ~310). This expansion signifies a substantial
      increase in market volatility and larger price swings, especially evident
      during the rapid upward movement and subsequent sharp decline in
      mid-December.
* **ATR (Average True Range):** The ATR quantitatively measures the degree of
  price volatility.
    * ATR values have fluctuated, indicating periods of both moderate and high
      volatility. Beginning around 12-14 in early September, the ATR rose
      markedly to a peak of 18.40 on 2025-12-17. This surge in ATR coincided
      precisely with the period of aggressive price action and increased daily
      trading ranges in mid-December.
    * The current ATR of 15.86 on 2025-12-24, although slightly down from its
      recent peak, confirms that AVGO is operating in a higher volatility
      environment compared to several months prior. This implies that while
      potential daily gains or losses can be larger, traders must implement
      robust stop-loss strategies.

AVGO presents a complex picture of a stock in a powerful long-term uptrend

  MACD crossing back decisively above its signal line into positive territory,
